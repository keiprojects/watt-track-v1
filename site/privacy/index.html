Watt Track Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 22, 2026

Watt Track is a local-first solar tracking app for manually recording household solar, grid, exported energy, savings, costs, and payback estimates.

Data Stored on Your Device

Watt Track stores the following information locally on your device:

  • Solar system profile, including optional location, optional site coordinates, rates, and reading modes.
  • Energy readings, calculated usage, calculated savings, notes, and validation warnings.
  • System costs, categories, descriptions, amounts, cost treatment, and notes.
  • App settings, display preferences, onboarding state, and optional daily reminder preferences.
  • Local backup restore points created when you choose to create a backup.

This data is stored using device-local app storage. Watt Track does not require an account and does not sync this data to a Watt Track server.

Data Sent Off Device

Watt Track may send saved location text, saved site coordinates, or a default location to Open-Meteo services to show current weather on the Home screen. This is used only to fetch weather context for the app experience.

The app does not use GPS location permissions.

Backups and Exports

Watt Track lets you create local backup restore points, CSV exports, and JSON backup files. After an export is created, you control where the file is saved or shared through your device's file and sharing tools.

Notifications

Watt Track may ask for notification permission if you enable daily reading reminders. Reminders are scheduled locally on your device and are used to remind you to enter readings.

Data We Do Not Collect

Watt Track does not collect names, email addresses, account credentials, advertising IDs, contacts, payment information, analytics, advertising, or tracking data.

Data Sharing

Watt Track does not sell user data and does not share app data for advertising or tracking. Weather requests may be sent to Open-Meteo when weather is displayed. Exported files may leave the app only when you choose to save or share them using your device.

Data Deletion

You can delete local app data, including local backup restore points, from inside Watt Track by using the reset or clear data controls. Exported CSV or JSON files are outside Watt Track's control after you save or share them.
